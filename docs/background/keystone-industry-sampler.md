# Keystone Industry Sampler

Geoff, this file exists to make one of the questions in `questions-for-geoff.md` easier to answer concretely.

You were asked: what industry or vertical do you recruit in most? The schema does not change with the answer. What changes is what Keystone Recruiting's example data looks like, the kind of job requisitions it carries, and which candidate attributes feel realistic next to placeholder values like email and phone.

Below is a one-paragraph sketch for each industry you might pick, plus one mixed option. Each sketch describes who Keystone's clients would be, what a typical Job Requisition looks like, what candidate attributes feel normal to fill in, what Source Channels appear, and what a Placed and a Rejected journey would tell. Pick whichever sketch reads closest to your real practice. If your practice is something not on the list (energy, education, government contracting, hospitality, transport, etc.), tell us and we will write a sketch for it.

## Technology

Keystone's clients are SaaS companies and venture-funded startups, with a handful of mid-market product engineering teams. A typical Job Requisition reads like "Senior Backend Engineer, Payments" or "Staff Site Reliability Engineer". The requisition carries seniority level (junior through staff), tech stack (Go, Python, Kubernetes, AWS), employment type (full-time, contract-to-hire), remote policy, and target compensation range. Candidate records carry resume URL, LinkedIn handle, GitHub handle, current employer, years of relevant experience, and stack tags. Source Channels are LinkedIn Recruiter, GitHub sourcing, employee referral, inbound applicants from the firm's job board, and a small number from technology meetups. A Placed journey shows a Senior Engineer sourced via LinkedIn, screened, taken through three interview loops, an offer, and an active Placement at a Series B startup. A Rejected journey shows a Staff candidate withdrawn after the offer stage when their counter-offer from current employer matched. Compliance overtones are minimal beyond standard EEO disclosure on application forms.

## Healthcare

Keystone's clients are hospitals, regional clinic networks, and travel-staffing agencies. A typical Job Requisition reads like "Registered Nurse, ICU, Night Shift" or "Medical Laboratory Technologist". The requisition carries licensure required (RN, MLT, etc.), license state, shift (day, night, swing, weekend, on-call), unit (ICU, ER, Med-Surg, Lab), employment type (per-diem, contract, permanent), and start date. Candidate records carry license number reference, license state, license expiration, certifications (ACLS, PALS, BLS), years of relevant experience, and shift preferences. Source Channels are licensed-nurse job boards, agency referrals, hospital direct-applicants, recruiter outreach to nurse associations, and reference check responses. A Placed journey shows an RN sourced from an agency referral, screened for license verification, interviewed by the unit manager, offered a 13-week travel contract, and started at a regional hospital. A Rejected journey shows a candidate disqualified at the screening stage when the license verification turned up an expired credential. Compliance overtones are moderate-to-significant: licensure verification, immunization records, background check chain of custody, and credentialing files. Some of this lives in a future compliance domain rather than Core.

## Construction

Keystone's clients are general contractors, electrical and mechanical subs, and a few civil engineering firms. A typical Job Requisition reads like "Journeyman Electrician, Commercial" or "Project Superintendent, Tilt-Up". The requisition carries trade (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, finish carpentry, ironworker), classification (apprentice, journeyman, foreman, super), union or non-union, project location, project duration (weeks to months), per-diem availability, and pay rate. Candidate records carry trade, classification, union affiliation, OSHA card status, certifications (OSHA 30, scissor lift, forklift, welding tickets), years in trade, willingness to travel, and per-diem requirement. Source Channels are union halls, trade school referrals, walk-ins, prior placements, and a small amount of online sourcing. A Placed journey shows a journeyman electrician walking in based on a prior placement, screened for current OSHA 30, interviewed by a project superintendent, offered a 16-week project, and started on a commercial tilt-up. A Withdrawn journey shows a candidate who took a competing offer with better per-diem before Keystone could close. Compliance overtones are moderate: OSHA card verification, drug screening (often required by client), and union ticket reciprocity across states.

## Finance

Keystone's clients are wealth-management firms, mid-market accounting practices, regional banks, and a few buy-side and sell-side advisory shops. A typical Job Requisition reads like "Senior Financial Analyst, FP and A" or "Wealth Management Associate, CFP track". The requisition carries function (FP and A, audit, tax, advisory, wealth), seniority (analyst, senior, manager, director), licensure (Series 7, Series 65, CPA, CFP, CFA candidate), industry exposure (banking, insurance, asset management), employment type, and target compensation range including bonus structure. Candidate records carry licensure list with status and expiration, current designation progress (CPA candidate, CFA Level 2), undergraduate institution, current employer, years of relevant experience, and target comp. Source Channels are recruiter outreach to LinkedIn, alumni networks of target schools, internal referrals, prior placement networks, and a very small amount of inbound. A Placed journey shows a Senior FP and A candidate sourced via alumni network, screened, taken through a case-study round, an offer, and a placement at a wealth-management firm. A Rejected journey shows a candidate who failed the case-study round on an out-of-vertical industry-specific question. Compliance overtones are significant for licensed roles: FINRA registration transfers, U4 disclosure history, background screening that meets industry standard. Some of this is regulator-specific and belongs in a future compliance domain.

## Mixed (general staffing)

Keystone's clients are mid-market employers across multiple industries, with no single specialty. Typical Job Requisitions cover a wide range: an office manager opening at a regional law firm, a controller at a small manufacturer, a service-desk lead at a managed-services provider, a clinical office coordinator at a multi-specialty practice. The requisition carries industry, function, seniority, employment type, and on-site or hybrid policy. Candidate records carry general resume, LinkedIn handle, target functions and industries, years of experience, and salary expectations, with industry-specific attributes captured loosely as text rather than as typed lookups. Source Channels are LinkedIn, the firm's job board, employee referrals, walk-ins, and prior placements coming back into the market. A Placed journey shows an office-manager candidate sourced via a prior placement's referral, screened, interviewed by the firm's managing partner, offered, and started. A Rejected journey shows a candidate withdrawn at the interview stage after a culture-fit mismatch. Compliance overtones are minimal at the Core level beyond EEO disclosure; industry-specific compliance lives downstream in the relevant client engagement, not in Core.
